Anime Pilgrimage

📍 Shima Onsen, Gunma

Sekizenkan Ryokan

★ Spirited Away · Real-Life Bathhouse Inspiration

Cross the red bridge at night and you are inside Spirited Away. Sekizenkan is Japan's oldest continuously operating ryokan — built in 1691 — and the direct visual inspiration for Yubaba's bathhouse. The lantern-lit corridors, steaming baths, and ancient wooden architecture make this the most sacred pilgrimage in all of anime tourism.

📍 Matsuyama, Ehime

Dogo Onsen Honkan

★ Spirited Away · Japan's Oldest Hot Spring

Japan's oldest bathhouse and a UNESCO candidate for world heritage. Hayao Miyazaki drew upon Dogo Onsen's 3,000-year history when creating the Spirit Bathhouse in Spirited Away. Arrive at dusk when the wooden building glows orange against the sky — a scene ripped directly from the film and burned into your memory forever.
